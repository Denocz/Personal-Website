Personal Development

The book Twelve Pillars by Jim Rohn and Chris Widener is actually an exciting book. Reading chapers 4 through to 7 really hit a few
words as it thought me alot of things, things that I did not know about. From chapters 4-7 the book highlighted some key points that I
really took into cosiderations. Some of these key points include: Achieving your goals, the proper use of time, surrounding yourself
with the best people and being a life-long learner. In achieving your goals, the book looks at both long-term and short-term goals,
writing them down so that they can become a reality. Writing down your goals is the best way to thread the needle towards achieving
all the things that you want to achieve in life. The best way to do that is to start by changing your direction. Apart from that, the
book looks at the proper use of time, and this is how you control your time and your day and how you use your time to gear it towards
your goals. Do not waste your time and day and dont let time control you, you should be he one to control your time and day, using it
towards achieving something effective. Surrounding your self with the best people is the next thing the book looked at. When achieving
your goals, the people who you surround yourself with has a major impact on you as they often influence your attitude and that is what
helps us to achieving our goals 90% of the time. We should surround ourselves with persons who are successful or who has the mindset to
be better. In other words we should surround ourselves with individuals who push us to be something greater. Lastly on chapter 7, the
book talks about being a long-life learner. Being a formal learner and a self learner. Now a formal learner is an individual who goes to
college and university and then they get a job and after they get that job that becomes there livng and they dont try to teach themselves
anything new. While a self leaner is an individual who despite going to college and university they still aspire to gain new knowledge or
should I say they teach themselves. Hence the saying from the book "Formal education will make you a living. Self-education will make you
a fortune." We as individuals must continue to gain new knowledge and we can do so by reading plenty of books as books are a treasure that
holds the key to a man's success. Reading chapters 4-7 from Twelve Pillars, it really showed me a greater insight into life and I can apply
what I have learnt from the book by changing the direction that I am going or should I say change the approach that I am taking. First step
is to jot down my goals, secondly I intend to use my time wisely, thirdly I intend to assess my inner circle and lastly, I intend to continue
to be a self learner.


The name of the degree that I am working on is the Bachelor's Degree of Science in Computing. Three skills that I have aquired so far are
effective communication, effective time management and critical thinking skills.
